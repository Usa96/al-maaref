American Curriculum, UAE Ready Education

At Al Maaref International School, we deliver a rigorous American curriculum aligned to U.S. academic standards, tailored to meet the needs of our diverse student body in the UAE. Our educational philosophy fosters independent thinking, creativity, and real-world problem solving.

With a strong emphasis on both core academics and holistic development, our curriculum integrates innovation, technology, and personal growth across all grade levels—from Early Years to High School.

Our programs are designed to equip students with the knowledge, skills, and values they need to succeed at top global universities and thrive in an ever-changing world.

Early Years (Age 3 – 5)

Excited minds love learning in the early years. Children in the Early Years love coming to school, looking forward to having fun, learning and making friends. We provide a safe and welcoming environment where every child develops confidence and independence.

Our beautifully designed early years classrooms and outdoor learning spaces provide a stimulating environment where young children can explore, enquire and create. Our highly trained Early Years team use the English National Curriculum as the basis for planning and assessment.

There are seven areas of learning:

  • Personal, social and emotional development
  • Communication and language
  • Physical development
  • Literacy
  • Mathematics
  • Expressive arts and design
  • Understanding the world

Our approach shapes resilient, engaged learners who play, create and persist with confidence.

Elementary

The curriculum at AlMaaref school focuses on guiding young learners as they gain confidence in Reading, Writing, Mathematics, Science, and Engineering.

Students develop the foundational skills for critical thinking, research, innovation, and creative exploration under the guidelines of the academically rigorous California State Common Core standards.

All teaching and learning intentionally support students' holistic development by providing opportunities to engage in purposeful learning ignited by curiosity. We encourage our students to take risks, explore to develop their skills and knowledge, and be proud of their achievements.

For a personalized learning experience for our learners, our educators provide relevant instruction in small and large groups through different formats, including class meetings, collaborative learning, research groups, and hands-on projects utilizing our Makerspace lab and the library.

We also ensure to extend experiential learning outside the classroom through field trips and community involvement. We link learning wherever possible to the national and international context and use opportunities to develop the diversity of our school community.

Middle School (Grades 6 – 8)

The AMPS Middle School incorporates design thinking and inquiry-based learning to encourage personalized learning experiences. Students begin a more intense preparation for their high school studies through multidisciplinary learning experiences in core and encore subjects like Math, English, Art, Science, and more.

Students are provided with daily learning challenges that promote their awareness as UAE and global citizens.

Academic Program:

The AMPS Middle School academic program is aligned with the California Common Core State Standards and MOE National Standards and follows a US Standards-Based grading scale. We are committed to offering a rigorous academic program that prepares students for high school, college, and career readiness.

Students engage in challenging learning experiences across language arts, science and engineering practices, mathematics, athletics, the arts, and music. AMPS ensures every student has a supportive environment throughout their middle school journey to foster both academic excellence and individual passions.

High School (Grades 9 – 12)

AMPS aims to prepare students for college and career in every phase of their academic program, with an increased experiential focus in the High School phase. Students are expected to be more independent in their learning through creating innovative products and internalizing their roles as changemakers and role models within the school community.

Design thinking projects are integrated into the curriculum, enabling students to develop solutions that positively impact local and global communities, while also enhancing creativity, critical thinking, and social skills.

Learning extends beyond the classroom through extracurricular programs, service-learning opportunities, and participation in student organizations.

High School Academic Program

The AMPS High School academic track is a rigorous college preparatory program aligned with the California Common Core State Standards and MOE National Standards. Students are graded using a U.S. Standards-Based grading scale.

Students challenge themselves in core areas such as language, science and engineering practices, mathematics, athletics, arts, and music. Each student is supported in pursuing their passions by choosing electives and maintaining a voice and choice in shaping their academic journey.

Minimum Graduation Credit

Students must complete 28 credits over four years to graduate. Each year, students enroll in a minimum of six core courses:

  • English
  • Science
  • Math
  • Social Studies
  • Arabic
  • Islamic Studies

A broad range of electives and co-curricular activities—such as business, psychology, computer science, PE, and visual arts—allow students to further explore their interests and strengths.

Advanced Placement (AP)

AMPS offers six AP courses equivalent to first-year university subjects. Students receive a GPA boost and are eligible for equivalency by scoring 3 or higher on the College Board’s AP exams taken in May.

In addition, students in Grades 11 & 12 can take dual credit courses via partnerships with Skyline University, American University of Sharjah, and Ajman University.
